Ransomware Response Runbook

Summary
Ransomware encrypts files and demands payment for decryption, often after data exfiltration for double extortion. Indicators include mass file modifications with unusual extensions, ransom notes (README/HOW_TO_DECRYPT files), disabled backups or shadow copies, and spikes in CPU/disk activity. Rapid containment is critical to limit spread.

Detection
1. Alert on mass file rename/modification events, creation of ransom-note files, and deletion of Volume Shadow Copies (vssadmin delete shadows).
2. Watch for disabled security tooling, new admin accounts, and lateral movement over SMB/RDP.
3. Monitor for large outbound transfers preceding encryption, which indicate data exfiltration.

Containment (act immediately, in parallel)
1. Isolate affected hosts from the network (disable switch port, pull network, or quarantine via EDR) but do NOT power them off if memory forensics may be needed.
2. Disable affected user and service accounts; block known C2 domains/IPs.
3. Protect backups: take backup systems offline or read-only to prevent encryption of recovery data.

Eradication and Remediation
1. Identify the ransomware family and initial access vector (phishing, exposed RDP, unpatched VPN) and close it.
2. Rebuild infected hosts from clean images rather than cleaning in place; rotate all credentials, especially domain admin and service accounts.
3. Apply outstanding patches and enforce MFA on remote access before reconnecting systems.

Recovery and Post-Incident
1. Restore data from verified, offline backups after confirming the environment is clean.
2. Engage legal/leadership on disclosure and law-enforcement notification; do not pay ransom without executive and legal review.
3. Conduct a post-incident review, deploy immutable/offline backups, and tighten segmentation and EDR coverage.
